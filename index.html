Apollo - Photon Food

Current population is reliant on biomass fuels with 98.9% of the population using firewood for domestic energy. Over-harvesting of wood has resulted in rapid deforestation and land degradation, with future projections showing no vegetation by 2020 if no solution is found. Deforestation has spread to neighbouring host countries which has created social conflict between migrants and residents. Firewood has negative impacts on health and wellbeing as 85% of respiratory related deaths are due to biomass burning. Given this situation, there is a need to reduce the firewood consumption in cooking.

SOLUTION

Apollo is a parabolic shaped cooker, which helps to concentrate all sun rays to one small surface area.
These reinforced UV-rays produce heat and thus increase the temperature of the pot/pan. The cooker
will use materials that can be fitted together by the refugees themselves.

Benefits

The temperature can reach up to 200°C which is suitable for basis daily cooking.
Helps to reduce the usage of wood.
Time-efficient.
Sustainable by reducing 3.14 kg CO2 per meal.

Drawbacks

Focus point of sun may be harmful to eyes.
Seasonal cooker.
Does not work at night.
Requires training to build.

ALTERNATIVES

Solar cookers have been around for many years, hence there being different models that could be used,
our solution is a combination of components from each of these designs.

  • Panel Cooker

    Cons: expensive and moderate durability

    Pros: low light performance with speed and versatility

  • Tube-Shaped Cooker

    Cons: expensive and moderate durability

OBJECTIVES & CRITERIA

The team has set out a list of objectives and their corresponding
criteria, with performance measurements and targets to meet

  • FIREWOOD CONSUMPTION

  • Objectives

  • Reduce the amount of wood consumption which directly impact the rate of deforestation and land disrupting

  • Criteria

  • The current rate of woodfuel consumption is 20kg per household, predicting no vegetation in the surrounding area within the next 3 years

  • A continuous assessment of woodfuel demand and land degradation should be considered to evaluate the outcome of the project

  • COOKING EFFICIENCY

  • Objectives

  • Provide more support to the refugees to have access to wood and energy efficient cooking practice

  • Criteria

  • Currently only one third of the whole Bidibidi population has access to efficient cookstoves and cooking support


  • The plan is to extend the support to every household in the selected Zone 4 area, with monitoring to receive feedback from the users

  • HEALTH IMPROVEMENT

  • Objectives

  • Reduce the rate of respiratory health diseases that correlate with the woodfuel emission


  • Criteria

  • Carbon monoxide resulting from firewood burning is the reason causing 85% of respiratory hospitalization and death

  • The current rate of respiratory related diseases is 24%. With calculation of applying the solar solution, the rate is predicted to drop to as low as 4%
